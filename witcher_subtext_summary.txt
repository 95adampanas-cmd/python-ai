The Witcher series is not only about monsters and magic, but about the
hidden struggles within people and society. It quietly explores themes
of destiny, choice, and the illusion of neutrality in a world full of
moral conflict. The story reflects on how fear of the unknown often
turns humans into the true monsters. Through Geralt and Ciri, it
suggests that identity is shaped more by experience and relationships
than by birth. Beneath the fantasy, the books are a subtle reflection on
power, prejudice, and the search for meaning in a chaotic world.
